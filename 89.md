A retail company has structured its AWS accounts to be part of an organization in AWS Organizations. The company has set up consolidated billing and has mapped its departments to the following OUs: Finance, Sales, Human Resources (HR), Marketing, and Operations. Each OU has multiple AWS accounts, one for each environment within a department. These environments are development, test, pre-production, and production.  
  
The HR department is releasing a new system that will launch in 3 months. In preparation, the HR department has purchased several Reserved Instances (RIs) in its production AWS account. The HR department will install the new application on this account. The HR department wants to make sure that other departments cannot share the RI discounts.  
  
Which solution will meet these requirements?

- A. In the AWS Billing and Cost Management console for the HR department's production account turn off RI sharing.
- B. Remove the HR department's production AWS account from the organization. Add the account 10 the consolidating billing configuration only.
- C. In the AWS Billing and Cost Management console. use the organization’s management account 10 turn off RI Sharing for the HR departments production AWS account.
- D. Create an SCP in the organization to restrict access to the RIs. Apply the SCP to the OUs of the other departments.

## 🧠 Understanding the Problem

The retail company uses **AWS Organizations** to manage multiple accounts, grouped into **Organizational Units (OUs)** for departments (Finance, Sales, HR, Marketing, Operations). Each department's OU contains accounts for different environments (dev, test, pre-prod, **production**).

The **HR department's production account** has purchased **Reserved Instances (RIs)**. The core requirement is to **prevent RI sharing** of the HR department's RI discounts with **other departments/accounts** within the AWS Organization.

### Key Concepts

- **AWS Organizations:** A service that allows you to centrally manage and govern your environment as you grow and scale your AWS resources.
    
- **Consolidated Billing:** A feature of AWS Organizations where all accounts in the organization are billed under a single bill. **By default, cost benefits, including RI discounts, are automatically shared across all accounts in the consolidated billing family.**
    
- **Reserved Instances (RIs):** A billing discount applied to the use of On-Demand Instances in your account.

## 🛠️ Services Explanation

|**Service**|**Description & Relevance**|
|---|---|
|**AWS Organizations**|The overarching service used to structure the company's accounts and manage consolidated billing. It also hosts **Service Control Policies (SCPs)**.|
|**AWS Billing and Cost Management**|The console/service where billing preferences, RI sharing settings, and cost reports are managed. RI sharing is a **billing preference**, not a resource access control.|
|**Reserved Instances (RIs)**|The resource whose discount needs to be restricted. RIs are a **billing commitment**, not a physical resource; they apply discounts to _running instances_.|
|**Service Control Policies (SCPs)**|A type of organization policy that you can use to manage permissions in your organization. SCPs are used to control **permissions** to AWS services/actions; they **cannot** control billing features like RI sharing.|

---

## ✅ Correct Option Explanation

**C. In the AWS Billing and Cost Management console. use the organization’s management account to turn off RI Sharing for the HR departments production AWS account.**

- **Official Documentation Reference:** AWS documentation on RI sharing states that **Reserved Instance discounts are shared across all accounts in an organization by default** when consolidated billing is in effect.
    
- **Mechanism:** The **AWS Management Account** (or a delegated administrator account) for the organization has the necessary permissions to manage consolidated billing preferences for all member accounts.
    
- **Solution:** To prevent a specific member account (HR production) from sharing its RI benefits with other accounts, you must **explicitly turn off RI sharing** for that account **in the Billing and Cost Management console**. This setting is managed at the organization level through the management account's billing configuration.

## ❌ Incorrect Options Explanation

| **Option**                                                                                                                                       | **Reason for Incorrectness**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **A. In the AWS Billing and Cost Management console for the HR department's production account turn off RI sharing.**                            | **Incorrect Permissions.** While the setting is correct, the **member account (HR production)** generally **does not have permission** to modify the consolidated billing preferences of the entire organization or its own sharing status. This action requires permissions in the **management account**.                                           |
| **B. Remove the HR department's production AWS account from the organization. Add the account to the consolidating billing configuration only.** | **Impractical and Incorrect Mechanism.** Once an account is removed from an Organization, it is a standalone account. You cannot "add it to the consolidating billing configuration only" without it being part of the Organization. This action would also lose other benefits of Organizations (SCPs, centralized management).                      |
| **D. Create an SCP in the organization to restrict access to the RIs. Apply the SCP to the OUs of the other departments.**                       | **Incorrect Service/Mechanism.** **SCPs control permissions** (what actions a user can take, e.g., launching an EC2 instance), but they **do not control billing preferences** like RI sharing. RI sharing is a **cost allocation/billing feature** set in the Cost Management console, not a resource access control that can be governed by an SCP. |

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

The correct and most direct way to control the sharing of Reserved Instance discounts within an AWS Organization is through the **Billing and Cost Management console** using the **management account's credentials**, where you can specifically disable RI sharing for the target member account.
